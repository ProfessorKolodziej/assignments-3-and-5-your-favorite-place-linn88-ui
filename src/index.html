New York City

New York City, known as "The City That Never Sleeps," is a mesmerizing blend of iconic landmarks, towering skyscrapers, and diverse neighborhoods, each offering unique cultural experiences. From the historical ambiance of Greenwich Village to the artistic streets of SoHo and the gourmet paradise of the Upper West Side, the city thrives on its dynamic energy and unmatched cultural scene.

Top attractions

  1. 1.Statue of Liberty and Ellis Island

    Symbolizing freedom and democracy, the Statue of Liberty is one of America's most iconic landmarks. A visit often includes a ferry ride to Ellis Island, where the Immigration Museum offers a poignant glimpse into the experiences of over 12 million immigrants who entered the United States through this portal.

  2. 2.Central Park

    An oasis amidst the city's skyscrapers, Central Park offers a peaceful retreat with its lush landscapes, walking paths, lakes, and outdoor activities. It's a place for relaxation, picnics, and taking in the natural beauty and architectural landmarks, such as Bethesda Terrace and the Central Park Zoo.

  3. 3.The Metropolitan Museum of Art

    Known as "The Met," it's one of the largest and most prestigious art museums in the world, offering a comprehensive collection that spans 5,000 years of art from across the globe. From ancient Egyptian artifacts to American and European masterpieces, The Met is a treasure trove for art lovers.

  4. 4.Broadway

    Experiencing a Broadway show is quintessential New York. The vibrant Theater District is home to numerous historic venues showcasing classic and contemporary plays and musicals, embodying the city's unparalleled performing arts scene.

  5. 5.Empire State Building

    An emblem of New York City, the Empire State Building offers breathtaking views from its observation decks on the 86th and 102nd floors. Visiting this iconic skyscraper provides a panoramic vista of the city's skyline and beyond, especially magical at sunset or night.

Top destination

The Metropolitan Museum of Art

Founded in 1870, The Met was established to bring art and art education to the American people. It opened its doors on Fifth Avenue in 1880 and has been expanding ever since. Its growth mirrors the city's own development into a global cultural capital. The museum's history is a testament to New York's status as a melting pot of cultures, with diverse exhibitions that reflect the city's multicultural heritage.
